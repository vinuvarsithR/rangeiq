Physics Model & Assumptions
Multi-factor physics-based consumption model
Every variable grounded in classical mechanics and EV drivetrain engineering. No curve-fit approximations.
Wh/km = [½ρC_dAv² + C_rr·m·g·cos(θ) + m·g·sin(θ)] / (η_drive × 3.6) + P_aux/v_kmh + W_stop × N_stops
         where W_stop = ½mv² × (1 − η_regen) / 3600
Aerodynamic drag
Drag force increases with the square of velocity. At 80 km/h, aerodynamic drag is 4× that at 40 km/h — the dominant loss factor at highway speeds.
F_aero = ½ × 1.225 × 0.45 × 0.45 × v²
         = 0.124 × v² N (v in m/s)
Rolling resistance
Constant force opposing motion due to tire deformation. Independent of speed, proportional to mass. Dominates at low speeds where aerodynamic drag is small.
F_roll = C_rr × m × g × cos(θ)
         = 0.018 × 200 × 9.81 ≈ 35.3 N
Stop-event KE loss
Each stop dissipates kinetic energy. Regen recovers η_regen = 44–68% depending on acceleration style. Stop/go traffic can add 15+ Wh/km from repeated cycles.
W_stop = ½mv² × (1 − η_regen)
η_regen ∈ {0.68, 0.62, 0.58, 0.52, 0.44}
